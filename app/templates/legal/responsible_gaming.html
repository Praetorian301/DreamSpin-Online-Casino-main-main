🎮 What is Responsible Gaming?

Responsible gaming means:

  • ✓ Only gambling what you can afford to lose
  • ✓ Setting time and money limits before you play
  • ✓ Never chasing losses
  • ✓ Taking regular breaks
  • ✓ Viewing gambling as entertainment, not income
  • ✓ Being honest with yourself about your gambling habits
  • ✓ Seeking help if gambling causes problems

⚠️ Warning Signs of Problem Gambling

You may have a gambling problem if you:

⚠️

Spend more money or time gambling than you intended

⚠️

Feel the need to be secretive about your gambling

⚠️

Have difficulty controlling or stopping your gambling

⚠️

Gamble when you can't afford to lose

⚠️

Family and friends are worried about your gambling

⚠️

Feel anxious, guilty, or depressed about gambling

⚠️

Chase losses by gambling more

⚠️

Neglect work, school, or family due to gambling

If any of these apply to you, please consider using our self-exclusion tools or seeking professional help.

💡 Tips for Staying in Control

Set a Budget

Before you start, decide how much money you can afford to lose. Never gamble with money needed for bills, rent, or other essentials.

Set Time Limits

Decide how long you'll play before you start. Use a timer or alarm to remind yourself when it's time to stop.

Never Chase Losses

Accept that losses are part of gambling. Don't try to win back what you've lost by gambling more.

Take Regular Breaks

Step away from the screen regularly. Take a walk, have a meal, or do something completely different.

Don't Gamble Under Influence

Avoid gambling when you're drinking alcohol or taking drugs. Your judgment is impaired.

Balance Your Life

Gambling shouldn't be your only hobby. Maintain other interests and social connections.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Information for Family and Friends

If someone you care about has a gambling problem:

  • Approach them with concern, not judgment
  • Listen without lecturing
  • Encourage them to seek professional help
  • Don't loan them money or pay their gambling debts
  • Consider seeking support for yourself - problem gambling affects families
  • Contact support organizations for guidance on how to help
